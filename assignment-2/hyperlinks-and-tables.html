What is URL Encoding

URLs can only use the ASCII character set, so any non-ASCII characters must be converted before they can be transmitted over the Internet. This conversion process, known as URL encoding, replaces non-ASCII characters with a "%" symbol followed by two hexadecimal digits.

URLs also cannot contain spaces. URL encoding handles spaces by replacing them with a plus sign (+) or with %20, ensuring that the URL remains valid and can be correctly interpreted by browsers and servers.

Example of an URL that needs encoding

https://example.com/search?query=Hello World!

The above URL contains a space (" ") and an exclamation mark ("!"), both of which need to be encoded to be transmitted correctly over the Internet.
