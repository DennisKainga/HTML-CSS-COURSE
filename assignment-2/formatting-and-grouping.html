HTML formatting tags

Five Commonly Used Tags

Here are five commonly used HTML formatting tags and explanations of their purpose, along with examples

  • (Strong Emphasis)

    The <strong> tag indicates strong emphasis or importance. Text inside is rendered in bold by default.

  • (Emphasized Text)

    The <em> tag gives text emphasis, which is often displayed as italic text. This tag is used to stress the importance or tone of the content.

  • (Superscript Text)

    The <sup> tag renders text as superscript, which appears slightly above the normal text line. It is commonly used for mathematical expressions, footnotes, and chemical formulas.

  • (Subscript Text)

    The <sub> tag renders text as subscript, which appears slightly below the normal text line. It is often used in mathematical expressions or chemical formulas.

  • (Code Snippet)

    The <code> tag is used to define a piece of computer code. It is typically displayed in a monospaced font.

Inline Elements

Inline elements are like small items that fit inside the big boxes. They only take up as much space as they need. They do not start on a new line. Instead, they flow within the same line of text.
